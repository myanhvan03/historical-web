# Who is Brendon Grimshaw?



Brendon Grimshaw (1925–2012) was an English journalist and conservationist best remembered for transforming Moyenne Island in the Seychelles into a protected paradise. Born in Dewsbury, Yorkshire, he began his career in newspapers, becoming Britain’s youngest chief reporter at 23 before editing publications in East Africa.

In 1962, Grimshaw purchased Moyenne Island for £8,000. At the time, it was wild and uninhabited. With the help of Seychellois companion René Antoine Lafortune, he spent decades restoring its ecosystem. They planted over 16,000 trees, reintroduced bird species, and nurtured a thriving population of giant tortoises.

His dedication ensured the island was preserved from commercial development. Eventually, Moyenne was declared part of the Sainte Anne Marine National Park, recognized as the smallest national park in the world. Grimshaw chronicled his journey in *A Grain of Sand* (1996), and his story inspired documentaries and global admiration.

He died in July 2012, leaving behind a living legacy of conservation.
